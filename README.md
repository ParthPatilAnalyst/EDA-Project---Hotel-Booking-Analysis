# Hotel Booking Analysis

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Using Python

## Project Description

This project presents a detailed **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of hotel booking data with the objective of understanding **customer booking behavior**, **cancellation patterns**, and **factors affecting hotel revenue and occupancy**.

In the hospitality industry, booking cancellations are a critical challenge. Even a small increase in cancellations can lead to significant revenue loss, poor room utilization, and inaccurate demand forecasting. This project uses Python-based data analysis techniques to help hotels make informed, data-driven decisions.

The analysis transforms raw booking data into structured insights that can support **pricing strategies, cancellation policies, seasonal planning, and capacity management**.

---

## Business Context

Hotels operate in a highly competitive and demand-sensitive environment. Frequent booking cancellations cause:

* Revenue uncertainty
* Inefficient room allocation
* Overstaffing or understaffing
* Poor long-term planning

Understanding *when*, *why*, and *who* cancels bookings is essential for improving operational efficiency and maintaining profitability.

---

## STAR Method Breakdown

### Situation

The hospitality industry experiences a high volume of booking cancellations across different seasons, customer segments, and booking channels. These cancellations directly affect:

* Daily and seasonal revenue planning
* Room occupancy rates
* Workforce scheduling
* Inventory and pricing decisions

Without proper analysis, hotels rely on assumptions rather than evidence to manage cancellations.

---

### Task

The main objectives of this analysis were to:

* Identify clear patterns and trends in booking cancellations
* Understand customer booking behavior across different segments
* Analyze factors influencing hotel revenue and occupancy
* Provide insights that support better forecasting and planning

The goal was to use data to reduce uncertainty in decision-making.

---

### Action

A structured and methodical Exploratory Data Analysis process was applied using Python.

#### Data Cleaning and Preparation

* Identified and handled missing values in important columns
* Corrected incorrect data types for dates and numerical fields
* Standardized categorical variables for accurate comparison
* Ensured data quality before performing analysis

#### Exploratory Analysis

* Performed **univariate analysis** to understand individual variable distributions such as:

  * Lead time
  * Number of guests
  * Booking status
* Conducted **bivariate analysis** to study relationships between key variables

#### Cancellation Behavior Analysis

* Analyzed the relationship between **lead time and booking cancellations**
* Identified that bookings made far in advance had a higher probability of cancellation
* Studied cancellation trends across:

  * Countries
  * Room types
  * Customer segments

#### Seasonal and Trend Analysis

* Examined booking and cancellation patterns across different seasons
* Identified peak seasons with higher booking volume and cancellation rates
* Compared weekday versus weekend booking behavior

#### Data Visualization

* Created clear and informative visualizations using:

  * Matplotlib
  * Seaborn
* Used bar charts, line plots, and distribution plots to communicate insights effectively

---

### Result

The analysis delivered strong and practical insights:

* Identified a **strong relationship between long lead times and higher cancellation rates**
* Discovered **seasonal peaks** in both bookings and cancellations
* Found **specific customer segments** with consistently higher cancellation behavior
* Generated insights that can help hotels:

  * Reduce cancellations through targeted policies
  * Improve revenue forecasting accuracy
  * Optimize room pricing and availability
  * Improve operational planning during peak seasons
